Disciplines

This project is a UX redesign of the Google Nest Thermostat system (device + app), aimed at creating an intuitive, data-driven experience that makes energy saving feel personal while enhancing everyday smart home use.

Challenge

How to turn complex energy data and system constraints into an intuitive, trustworthy smart home experience that everyday users can understand and act on without anxiety or effort.

My Role As The UX Designer

As the team lead, I led the end-to-end UX design process — from user research to task flows, wireframes, and prototypes — and established a cross-platform design style guide. I also collaborated with the team on social-facing materials, including a magazine, posters, and lookbooks.

Working Highlight
HMW

How Might We Make Energy Savings Visible And Understandable In Thermostat Interactions, Enabling Users To Balance Comfort And Cost With Confidence?

Design Process

Through early foundational and usability research, we identified friction points in the thermostat's core workflows — especially around scheduling and real-time temperature control — while also establishing a clear visual direction.
