——— 2. OVERVIEW 概述 ———
I removed the notification banner that previously dominated the first screen, and replaced it with more compelling video thumbnails, larger and more prominent CTAs. The core goal is to hook users as quickly as possible using video imagery.

My role
As the sole designer of Electronic Direct Mails, collaborating with PMs to define visual and content strategies to increase user engagement

Timeline

March – May 2024

Skills

——— 3. CONTEXT ———
Context
Email has become a popular marketing tool. U.S. users are overwhelmed with "useless" marketing emails and looking for useful and relevant email content.
Over 30% of surveyed users are dissatisfied with useless and irrelevant content.

——— 4. GOAL ———
Goal
Business goal: Email notifications as a standalone, value-driven content and marketing channel.
User goal: Receive more personalized and meaningful content. Improve upon its value to compete as an independent marketing channel.

——— 5. PROBLEM BREAKDOWN ———
Problem breakdown — Why does everyone hate email notification?

Strategic level
Not treated as an independent product experience. Lack of audience segmentation strategy.

Content level
No differentiated content strategy by engagement level & identity type.

Visual & interaction
Weak visual cues, lack of storytelling.

"Stop sending me a million emails telling me I have 75, 100 etc notifications. Trust me I don't. I'm on the app daily." — CA user

Heavy users perceive emails as redundant. Creators (57.5% CA, 66.8% BR) report [Create and share my own videos] as a main reason for using TikTok — they expected more relevant topics.

"Actually my email always appear the notification but I directly go in TikTok instead open the email. When I check my emails, I also see the email of TikTok and I also check and directly to TikTok after my other email done checking." — TT users

~46% users in both countries go to the TikTok app directly instead of checking content via email.

Visual & interaction painpoint: 22.6% BR users don't realise the email content is clickable. Unclear CTAs and no narrative flow. Repetitive content that doesn't add value wastes valuable vertical space.

——— 6. HIGHLIGHTS ———
Highlights — Visual improvement
Capture user attention in the shortest amount of time using video thumbnails with personally relevant content.

Design decision
Remove the notification section
Text-heavy, low visual impact. Adding personalized information alongside video tags to provide more useful context. Enlarged play button for immediately recognizable clickability. Added social CTA buttons and amplified the people info section to drive curiosity.

Before / After

Design directions
Option 1 — Scenario: People and social relationship-driven
Pros: Added social CTA buttons, amplified the people info section, de-emphasized video content to build curiosity. Best suited for social-first post types: friend posts, suggested friends, follow posts, and interactive posts.
Cons: Not ideal for pure content recommendation scenarios where the content itself takes center stage.

Option 2 — Scenario: Video Cover First / Content-Driven
Pros: Best suited for content-forward post types like suggested videos and reposts. Prioritizes the video thumbnail, with de-emphasized people info.
Cons: Not ideal for posts with a strong social context.

Option 3 — Scenario: Promotional-style email
Pros: High visual impact, drives immediate action, works well for campaign launches, sales, and event announcements. Prioritizes bold visuals and strong CTAs over social context or personalization.
Cons: Feels less personal, lower relevance for users not already interested, risk of being perceived as spam.

——— 9. TAKEAWAYS ———
Something you can't learn in a class

01 — I realized I could contribute more than just visual execution
Working on this project taught me that as a designer — even as an intern — you can and should engage with the product direction, not just the visual execution. When the initial brief felt unclear, asking questions and understanding the "why" behind the design helped me make more intentional decisions.

02 — Looking back at existing data and past design iterations gave me a lot of context
For a product that had gone through many iterations, I found it incredibly valuable to dig into existing data, past design files, and how previous designers had approached similar problems. It gave me context I couldn't have gotten from the brief alone, and helped me arrive at a more informed direction rather than starting from scratch.
